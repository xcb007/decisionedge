The Problem

Most Excel training prepares you for tests, not decisions.

If you've spent hours on tutorials but still hesitate when a real business question lands on your desk, you're not alone.

Feature-first, not problem-first

You learn VLOOKUP before you understand what question it answers. Tools without context don't build judgment.

No business model thinking

Courses teach spreadsheets, not how a business actually works. You end up with pretty models with no managerial insight.

Too basic for advanced users

Online courses reset to zero. If you already know the basics, you waste hours before getting to anything useful.

No real-world case depth

Toy datasets don't build confidence. Real decisions involve messy data, ambiguous questions, and stakeholder pressure.
